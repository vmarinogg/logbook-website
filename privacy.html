Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

What data we collect

Training data. Your workout sessions, exercises, sets, reps, loads, and notes are stored locally on your device and synced to your personal iCloud account. We never access, read, or process your training data on our servers.

Apple ID. When you sign in with Apple, we receive your name and email (or Apple's relay email). This is stored locally on your device for identification purposes only.

Program descriptions. When you set up a training program, the text or file you provide is sent to a third-party service (Groq) for parsing into structured data. This text is not stored by us or by Groq after processing.

Website analytics. The Logbook website uses PostHog to understand page visits, campaign parameters, performance, heatmaps, and App Store link clicks. We do not use this to read your training data.

What we don't collect

  • No in-app analytics for your training data
  • No advertising identifiers
  • No location data
  • No health data beyond what you manually enter
  • No social data or contacts

Where your data lives

Your training data is stored on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework. If you enable iCloud sync, your data is also stored in your personal iCloud account, encrypted by Apple. We have no access to your iCloud data.

Third-party services

Groq API. Used solely for parsing program descriptions into structured training plans. Text is sent via HTTPS and not retained after processing. See Groq's privacy policy.

Apple StoreKit. Used for in-app purchases. Managed entirely by Apple.

PostHog. Used for website analytics and campaign measurement. Website events may include page URL, referrer, campaign parameters, device/browser context, and App Store link clicks.

Data deletion

You can delete all your data by deleting the app from your device. If you use iCloud sync, you can also delete your data from iCloud via Settings → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage Storage.
